[The art of rest](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAZwy34BqJczZe0-4EV-UcCmpRfzJCI_VE4)[Claudia Hammond](https://www.linkedin.com/in/claudia-hammond-0a623030/).

An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management ([Will Larson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAC0KdQBkikUIfCPY2nyso3lWQqRKXj_ViQ)). This book really resonates with the engineer in me and gives a hands-on perspective on engineering management. I really like the first half of the book that is more practical and visual and with good examples.

Difficult conversations: How to discuss what matters most (Douglas Stone, Bruce Patton, [Sheila Heen](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAEnfxoB_iyHhPxdgNHjeCYM7SxXLRAE-s8) ). This is a great book for anyone interested in improving their ability to manage conflicts, negotiate, give feedback or just listen effectively and manage tough conversations. I wish this was mandatory read at all workplaces.  
  
Driving Data Quality with Data Contracts ([Andrew Jones](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAFSsk0BKJDmlzMve0Siaq17uORJBzkwPBw) ). This book is a good introduction to data contracts and confirms a lot of what we have implemented at Mathem. It not only covers the benefits of data contracts but also offers practical guidance and examples. A must read for every data engineer considering implementing data contracts.
