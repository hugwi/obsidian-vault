Hip pain during approaches or landings in bouldering can be a challenge, often caused by compressive forces irritating hip structures. Long-term, the best approach is strengthening the hip and improving movement patterns. But for immediate relief, hip traction might help. Always seek advice from a physical therapist first.

Lying Down Traction  

1️⃣ Lie down with your leg elevated on a bolster or pillow.  

2️⃣ Attach a resistance band to your ankle, with the other end anchored, creating gentle traction.  

3️⃣ Relax and let the band gently pull your leg, relieving pressure in the hip.  

4️⃣ Start with short durations, around 30 seconds, and gradually increase.

Standing Traction  

1️⃣ Stand on one leg, preferably on a step. Can use the railing or a dowel for balance.  

2️⃣ Attach a light weight (3-5 pounds) to your other ankle.  

3️⃣ Let the weighted leg dangle off the step, creating a traction effect.  

4️⃣ Hold for 30 seconds to 5 minutes, starting with shorter periods.

Remember, these are temporary solutions. Consulting a PT for personalized advice and focusing on strengthening and improving hip movement are key for long-term relief.
