#### My BCF: Getting the Todd Crush

*By **Karin Hartzell**, Consultant, Stockholm* 

It had been a tough year in the Candy Crush Saga team at King, makers of the world's largest mobile game. Despite numerous efforts and investments, we were not finding our way to growth. Two from-the-top reorganisations had failed to improve things, projects were started – then abandoned, and high performers started to walk out the door. I was on my way out too sadly, having tried all I could think of to connect with the leadership team and have a dialogue on how I could help turn things around. Unfortunately, without succeeding.

But on my way out, a few things happened that did turn things around, both for the game and for me personally. Next level leadership also had enough and decided to replace the current “Head of” with a trusted person known to create results. This person was **Todd Green** and they convinced him to move his young family from London to Sweden for a second time.

I saw Todd on stage in an all-hands meeting during my last week and thought *“He seems like a great person and leader. What a shame that we will not get to work together.”* And for some reason I decided to go out of my comfort zone and share this observation with him. Perhaps the fact that it was my last week made me braver than usual. So right after that all-hands, I approached this senior leader I had never met before with this feedback:

*“Hey, just wanted to say you are an incredibly good communicator. Your talk today gave a strong impression of presence and authenticity that the team really needs right now.”* 

**“How come you are leaving?”** 

The message was appreciated but more importantly, it opened for longer conversation. Todd asked about my time in the team, my thoughts on why things were not going well and what caused their big projects to fail. It was clear that he was curious and wanted to listen, also when I mentioned the many challenges I saw. It seemed like my observations resonated with things he heard elsewhere. At the end he said:

*“So this is your last week in the team? How come you are leaving? Are you sure about that decision? I think you should come and work directly with me and help me turn this around instead. No need to decide on the spot but think it over and I will call you next week to discuss what options we have.”* 

Somehow this out-of-my-comfort-zone-move built trust and opened for an opportunity way beyond my imagination. A ticket straight to the centre of action where my opinions and my experience made ten times more impact than in any of my previous positions. I decided to stay of course, and in the three years that followed, I worked closely with Todd and a small, trusted group to identify root causes of the organisational problems and solve them one by one. The team grew by 40% and revenues almost doubled – in a game that makes millions of dollars every day.

**The power of unleashing client potential** 

The senior leadership team who urged Todd to come back knew what they were doing – Todd was clearly the right person for the job. Hard working and professional but also playful, human and relatable. Open to new knowledge and perspectives including feedback on his own weak points. Never afraid to make tough decisions and there were for sure many of those in the following organisational overhauls. His high standards made him quite demanding to work with, but he also showed trust and established a safety that enabled bold moves. We joke that everyone falls into a starry-eyed “Todd Crush” when they meet him – I guess that includes me as well.

The powerful return to success of Candy Crush Saga led by Todd set him on a steep career trajectory to a mega influential leader in the company. It was a career game changer for me too and a true horse and rider experience. I am forever grateful for this chance to help unleash amazing potential in Todd and in the Candy Crush Saga Team.

**The First Impact Stories Workshops in Stockholm**

By **Henric Wirfelt**, ES, Stockholm

What you do describes who you are, doesn't it? If that’s true, would describing snapshots of what we do together describe who Netlight is? The answer is most likely no. What you do does not fully describe who you are, but it gives the listener an opportunity to better understand what drives you and what you're passionate about.

Over my ten years at Netlight, I’ve probably been in more than 5000 client meetings and described who we are and what we do in more than 5000 different ways. Sometimes, I focused on our strong culture that binds us together, and other times I related to the fantastic clients and people outside Netlight whom we work with.

For some time now, we have spent a lot of effort to put into print what we do and how we do it. It could be seen as part of Netlight's IP, Netlight's Structure Capital. However, to me, the journey to define and put into print what we do has been a vital exercise in reflection. It has created an understanding of where we are, and where we need to go, why, and for whom. Perhaps for the world?

This week, I’ve been involved in arranging the first Impact Story workshops in Stockholm. What we now call impact stories (our reference cases/client success stories) have a long history at Netlight and now have a new format, structure, and organization behind them. **The concept, Impact Stories, was first introduced in January 2023**, **launched in February in #nl-announcements,** and we’ve held many successful workshops in Munich, spreading the understanding of the concept, creating stories to be shared in a structured way, improving quality and consistency in how we communicate what we do externally.

The Impact Story Workshop Framework is designed to not only produce high-quality texts that are easily accessible for everyone, with nice filters and GDPR compliance but also to give the delivery teams a session to reflect on what they’ve been working on, why, and what value it has created for their clients. For two workshops, we gathered 80 people who work with 15 different clients, in different industries, and with different challenges. The content we created together is now ready to be shared outside (and inside) Netlight to show the world what we are working on and to do it proudly and with confidence!

I'm super happy to be involved in this movement and to work with all the great people who make this happen! More will follow. Check out our Sharepoint space [Impact Stories](https://netlight365beta.sharepoint.com/sites/impact-stories) and join us at **#impact-stories**.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
