#### [Batholomy](https://www.reddit.com/user/Batholomy/)

 (2024-04-26 17:12:39)

I'm 52 and climb with OA in one knee. My knee has lost its last 30-45 degree of flexion and does a stabby pain thing if I land on it in an awkward fashion. But I climb 3-4 times a week. Mostly bouldering. Stretching hamstrings and gluts really helps. I downclimb when I can. If I jump off, I always land on my other leg. All up, climbing has really helped with strength and flexibility in both my legs and has been good for my OA overall. For context, I climb around V5 and climb for fun rather than train intensively.

With two OA hips I would suggest lots of stretches (get a PT to modify stretches for your OA), climbing down rather than jumping off, and consider top rope or lead climbing rather than bouldering (to avoid jumping down). I have a friend with two hip joint replacements who still climbs but only top ropes.

Climbing is just fun! But it is also definitely good for strength and flexibility. And it's nice hanging off your arms when your legs are shit!

#### [LuckyMacAndCheese](https://www.reddit.com/user/LuckyMacAndCheese/)

 (2024-04-26 18:12:21)

You could try adding some yoga into your mix. Helps greatly with general strength/flexibility/balance/endurance, and there are a lot of hip opening/stretching yoga poses. I have an autoimmune arthritis in my hand and upper back and live in a colder climate, doing a gently heated yoga session when I'm feeling stiff/sore particularly in the colder months has been really very nice... And I think the better balance/flexibility has helped some with my climbing.

I do admit, I now almost exclusively do roped climbing. Occasionally I'll do a very easy boulder as a warmup but the fall risk is something I think about a lot and have decided it's just not really worth it to me.
