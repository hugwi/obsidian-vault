**Limited and costly analytics & ML** 
You’re restricted to Salesforce’s analytics, ML and AI capabilities, and anything advanced typically comes with high licensing and integration costs.

**Data ownership & compliance risk**  
It can be harder to comply with GDPR. Customer data often exists in many objects, histories, logs, backups, and integrations. Ensuring complete deletion is non-trivial.

**Customization**
- Either require more effort to customise to business processes or change business processes 

**Increased complexity**
- Every new system (ERP, marketing automation, analytics platform, support tools) may require custom adapters or middleware to communicate with Salesforce.
- Changes in Salesforce  can break integrations unexpectedly.

**Big bang integration risk**  
Migrating all customer data and processes to Salesforce at once is high risk. Data quality issues, incomplete mapping, or overlooked dependencies can break business operations. The larger and more complex the move, the harder it is to roll back, test, or fix mistakes. It can also overwhelm teams and slow adoption.
