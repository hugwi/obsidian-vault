Andreas
Netlight supported a leading re-commerce marketplace in their transition to Data Mesh to enable the company to create more value from their data. Netlight planned and implemented the necessary changes in the architecture and scoped the transition for the organizational structure. A vital part of the successful transition was establishing a strategy for effective decentralized computational data governance. It included designing and implementing clear processes and guidelines for data ownership, documenting data in a data catalogue, and introducing best practices and guidelines for data quality, including implementing data contracts to ensure that all teams adhere to arranged data quality standards and SLAs


Hugo
The client had experienced a rapid growth in the data domain, which consequently had led to growing issues with ungoverned data. To address these concerns Netlight supported the client with the primary objectives to:
- enhance data discoverability
- enable easier data access
- establish data accountability
- promote data literacy 
- data compliance 

Netlight supported in implementing a comprehensive data catalog encompassing metadata records for over 80,000 data tables, spanning both cloud-native and on-premises environments. Additionally, to meet GDPR requirements, Netlight assumed responsibility for developing and deploying a customised Personal Identifiable Information (PII) scanning solution, designed for the precise detection of PII within the dataset. Access policies pertaining to PII data were also formulated to ensure the security and compliance in the handling of sensitive information.
